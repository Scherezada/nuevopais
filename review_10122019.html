A Brief Comparison of Affordable Real Estate CRM Solutions for Real Estate Agents in Canada 

 

Last Update: December 10, 2019

Not surprisingly, one of the biggest mistakes of a new real estate business is to pick up the most advanced and, therefore, costly CRM. High cost, combined with the complexity of the product, usually makes users spend most of their time to figure out the advanced features to make their money worth the outcome. The customers typically end up with an all-purpose excel sheet and idle software in the cloud.

 

As a rule of thumb, your preferred CRM should address your needs and make up for its cost. It should also be scalable according to your organizational needs, and most importantly, you should be able to use it effectively.

 

We think that it would be wise to start with an affordable CRM solution and to make a decision in the following years whether to upgrade or not to a more advanced and costly one. Nowadays, it is generally effortless to convert and transfer data between several platforms. There is also a high chance that you can find the right product at the right price and keep using it forever. Online reviews take an essential place to have that chance; nevertheless, many of the reviews are biased and paid by CRM vendors.

 

That is why we decided to write our own review as a general guideline for new and experienced agents. But first, let’s start with some basics.

 

What is CRM? Why do real estate agents need one?

 

CRM Software (or Customer Relationship Management Software) is a software category that helps businesses build strong relationships with their customers. CRMs boost conversations and improve revenue rates based on customer data coming from multiple interaction channels (phone calls, website visits, social interactions, and so on).

 

Companies or individuals use them to process and analyze customer information, make use of detached data to grasp opportunities, generate sales funnels, and carry out loyalty campaigns. One of the most significant benefits of CRM software is that it allows you to categorize customers according to their expectations and give attention to prospective buyers.

 

Today, CRM developers are focused on delivering customizable and flexible CRM products and incorporating back-end analytic engines for generating relevant insights. It is possible to use most CRMs as communication systems, contact management platforms, lead generation apps, and even marketing tools. CRM’s are becoming more predictive, which turns them into competitive systems for navigating changes in all industries. Instead of a traditional ‘one size suits all’ CRMs, there will be more industry-specific CRM apps. This concept also applies to real estate businesses.

 

A real estate business usually starts with a pen and notebook, like many other businesses. As the notebook gets thicker, it becomes a real burden to follow customer leads and activities, to keep records of listings and deals, and even manage all the necessary paperwork. That is one reason a realtor should start using CRM as soon as possible, preferably starting from day one.

 

 

The features of CRM for your real estate business

 

There are tens, if not hundreds of CRM solutions commercially available in the market. One should very well know the priorities of the business. In our experience, high-cost usability rarely makes sense in any business activity. Nevertheless, ‘what costs nothing is worth nothing’ may not always be the case for real estate CRMs. Instead, we recommend taking the ‘take a second look; it costs you nothing’ approach. Complexity is another issue. It is useful that a CRM needs little configuration at the start, but it is probably more important to utilize it for long years.

 

Apart from the cost and usability, a quick list of essential items/features to be considered by a real estate agent or office when deciding on a CRM product could be:

 

· Contact Management

· Leads & Opportunity Management

· Create & Search Listings

· Task Management

· General Customization

· Deals & Contract Management

· Report Functions

· Team Permissions & Management

· Third-Party Integrations

· Marketing Management

· Other CRM Capabilities

 

We will elaborate more on the criteria to look for under each bullet item.

 

 

Our methodology for this review

 

 

There are many things we all would like to avoid for the test and evaluation of any software product, such as long registration forms, questionnaires, popup videos. It is one of the reasons we avoid reviewing many CRM solutions, which we shortlisted in the beginning. Of course, that does not mean that they are not good enough.

 

Below you may find common denominators for the CRM solutions we picked for this review:

 

·       Affordable price: We did not want to review the products that are not priced, in our opinion, reasonably. Affordability is a subjective term, and to give a better insight, we wanted to include a chart giving a summary of costs for the selected products. We also avoided reviewing the products with no clear pricing information on the product website. We know that they are all outstanding solutions, but in the end, we eliminated products such as:

 

https://propertybase.com/

https://premieragent.zillow.com/

https://propertymate.ai/

 

You can click here if you want to read a detailed review of ‘propertybase’ and many other real estate CRMs.

 

·       Easy registration and testing: Many of the products in the market ask for a lot of details like your real name, business phone and email address for the trial registration. A few of them even pushed us to schedule a meeting or a discovery call for the first introduction to the product. We were hesitant to talk over the phone on this issue. Firstly, our only objective was to test the product for the short term, and we did not want to take anyone’s precious time for such a purpose. Secondly, we don’t actually like ‘we want a meeting, but we promise we will not hassle you forever’ approach.

 

We excluded https://freeagentcrm.com/, although it is known to be an excellent estate CRM solution.

 

You can also click here or here for a review of ‘freeagent crm’.

 

·       Credit card information: We all like free stuff. The trial period is free. Why do we also need to prove our intention to buy?

 

Eliminated https://www.ixactcontact.com/ even though it seems like a very good, and all in one real estate CRM solution.

 

You can click here for a short review of ‘IXACT Contact’.

 

·       Industry-specific solution: This review is for the use of real estate agents and offices. We preferred to eliminate all-purpose CRMs that are built for every kind of business, of course, unless they are configured for the use of real estate businesses.

 

·       Canadian: Preferably based in Canada or doing business in Canada. After all, this review is for the real estate agents and offices that are active in Canada.

 

We have to mention that we did not test the products for the long term. We tested according to our own needs and for a short time only. For the comparison list, we used our test experience together with the information given on the product website, assuming that it reflects the product's real features and costs. We should also mention that we did not test customer services. In our experience, it usually takes a long time to understand the value of customer service. Unfortunately, many companies are very good at customer service at the start, but we cannot say the same after you got hooked.

 

Finally, these are just brief reviews, ideas and comments of our own. They may not suit your needs, and they may include mistakes, so please do not take them as a fact without actually testing the product.

 

The criteria and how did we score them

 

There are five categories under each feature, in which we used one full or half star as a score for each that satisfied our needs. We separately reviewed main functions, such as reporting if there was an added value for such a category.

 

Overall ratings are simply the arithmetic averages of each category, giving them equal weights. Knowing that they should have different weight factors for various needs, we also wanted to include a separate summary score for each feature.
